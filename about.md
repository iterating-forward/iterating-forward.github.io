## The Short Version

I've spent 15+ years in software — as an engineer, tech lead, Scrum Master, and Agile Coach. I've seen Agile done brilliantly and catastrophically. This blog is where I write about what actually works.

## The Longer Version

I started my career writing code. Then I led teams writing code. Then I spent years helping organisations figure out why their teams weren't shipping.

The pattern I kept seeing: smart people, good intentions, terrible process. Teams drowning in ceremonies that produced nothing. Scrum Masters running retrospectives nobody acted on. Agile transformations that transformed nothing except the job titles.

I also saw the opposite. Teams that shipped fast and stayed healthy. Leaders who understood that frameworks are starting points, not finish lines. Organisations that treated process as something to improve, not something to comply with.

The difference was rarely the framework. It was whether people understood *why* the practices existed — and had the judgment to adapt them.

## What I Write About

**Agile and Scrum** — not the certification-industrial complex version, but what actually helps teams deliver.

**Engineering culture** — why developers hate most implementations of Agile, and what to do about it.

**Leadership** — the organisational and political realities that nobody mentions in training courses.

**AI and teams** — how AI tools are changing what it means to develop software, and what that means for the people involved.

## Who This Is For

- Scrum Masters wondering if they're actually making a difference
- Engineers frustrated with process theater
- Leaders trying to figure out if Agile still works — or ever did

If you've ever sat through a ceremony thinking "this is a waste of everyone's time," you're in the right place.

## The Name

Iterating Forward. One step at a time. Ship something, learn from it, improve. That's the whole philosophy in two words.
