**Summary**: "Agentic AI" becomes the hottest buzzword in tech. Everyone's talking about autonomous agents. Almost nobody's actually using them effectively.

**Tech Context (May 2025)**: Deloitte predicts 25% of companies will launch agentic AI pilots in 2025. Web Summit Vancouver focuses on AI agents. Capital One and other enterprises announce agentic AI initiatives.

---

Last month, someone asked if we could replace our Sprint Planning with an AI agent. I laughed. Then I thought about it.

## What Agentic AI Actually Is

Agentic AI is real - but it's not magic. Autonomous agents that can:

- Plan and execute multi-step tasks
- Self-correct when things go wrong
- Operate without constant human supervision

The key insight: AI agents amplify your existing organizational capability.

## The Prerequisite Nobody Mentions

You can't automate what you haven't defined.

I spent a week with a team excited about deploying an AI agent to "handle their backlog." When I asked them to describe their refinement process, I got four different answers from four people. Their Definition of Done was a wiki page last updated in 2022.

The agent would have automated chaos.

**What agentic AI needs to work:**

Clear inputs. If your tickets are inconsistent, the agent produces inconsistent outputs. Garbage in, autonomous garbage out.

Defined authority. Who can the agent say "yes" to? What decisions require a human? Most teams can't answer this for their own members, let alone a piece of software.

Feedback loops. Agents learn from corrections. If nobody's reviewing outputs, nobody's improving the system. You'll just get bad decisions at scale.

**The uncomfortable truth:**

The teams ready for agentic AI are teams that already work well. They have clear processes, trust each other, and know how to delegate. The AI just extends what they can do.

The teams buying agentic AI tools are usually teams looking for a fix. They think autonomy is a product you purchase. It's not.

**A real example:**

Capital One's agentic AI initiative worked because they'd spent years on DevOps maturity. Their pipelines were clean. Their processes were documented. The agents had something coherent to plug into.

Meanwhile, a startup I advised bought the same vendor, gave it access to their Notion, and got an agent that scheduled meetings with itself.

## The Contrarian Take

The companies hyping agentic AI the most are the ones least prepared to use it. Autonomy isn't something you add with software.
