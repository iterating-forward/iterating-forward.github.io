**Summary**: Amazon, Dell, and AT&T announce full return-to-office mandates. This isn't just an HR story - it's fundamentally about trust, and Scrum's self-organizing teams are the canary in the coal mine.

**Tech Context (Feb 2025)**: Amazon's 5-day RTO mandate takes effect. Dell announces March RTO. Federal workers ordered back to office. 1 in 4 C-suite executives admit RTO policies were designed to encourage voluntary turnover.

---

Your daily standup just got harder. Not because of the work - because half your team is now commuting 3 hours a day.

## The Trust Problem

RTO mandates are a symptom of organizations that never trusted self-organizing teams in the first place. If you need to see butts in seats to believe work is happening, you never believed in Scrum.

The companies forcing RTO are the same ones where "Agile transformation" meant renaming meetings.

## What This Reveals

A quarter of C-suite execs admitted the quiet part out loud: RTO was designed to make people quit. That's not a workforce strategy. That's a layoff with extra steps and worse optics.

But here's what bothers me more than the mandate itself: the companies issuing them spent the last decade claiming to be Agile.

Amazon runs two-pizza teams. Dell talked up DevOps culture. These weren't waterfall holdouts. They had the ceremonies, the certifications, the transformation consultants. And the moment things got hard, they defaulted to "I need to see you working."

**What actually happened:**

The teams that tracked velocity and sprint goals kept delivering remotely. The teams that tracked "responsiveness on Slack" and "camera on during standups" panicked when they couldn't surveil anymore.

RTO isn't about collaboration. If it were, they'd mandate 2-3 days for co-located planning, not 5 days of commuting to sit on Zoom calls anyway.

**The self-organizing lie:**

"Self-organizing" was always conditional. Self-organize your tasks, sure. Self-organize your working hours? Your location? How you communicate? That was never on the table.

The Scrum Guide says teams should be empowered to choose how to do their work. Most orgs interpreted that as "choose which Jira workflow to use."

## The Contrarian Take

The real Agile companies? They're staying remote - because they actually measure outcomes, not attendance.
