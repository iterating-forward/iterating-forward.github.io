**Summary**: Microsoft reports GitHub Copilot now writes 30% of their code. Companies start citing AI for layoffs. But the cuts aren't coming for the roles you'd expect.

**Tech Context (Mar 2025)**: Microsoft layoffs hit 15,000 roles. CrowdStrike, Workday cite AI for workforce reductions. Microsoft CEO says Copilot writing 30% of new code.

---

Every Scrum Master I know is asking the same question: "Am I next?"

## What AI Actually Replaces

AI isn't replacing facilitation, coaching, or organizational change - it's replacing middle management. The irony: the roles most threatened are the ones that never let Scrum Masters actually do their jobs.

The layers of approval, the status meetings, the project managers tracking velocity - those are the roles AI eats first.

## The Real Threat

The threat isn't AI. It's the manager who thinks AI replaces the wrong things.

I watched a VP last month demo an "AI Scrum Master" tool to his leadership team. It generated sprint reports, flagged blockers from Jira, and sent automated standup summaries. He was genuinely proud.

Nobody in that room asked: "Who resolves the blockers? Who notices the dev going quiet because they're burned out? Who tells the PM their scope creep is killing the team?"

The AI didn't know. The VP didn't either. That's the problem.

**What AI actually automates well:**

Pulling data from Jira. Formatting burndown charts. Sending reminders. Writing status updates nobody reads. Summarizing retro notes.

These are tasks. Important ones, maybe. But they're not why good Scrum Masters exist.

**What AI can't touch:**

The conversation where you figure out the tech lead and product owner don't actually agree on what "done" means. The coaching session where a junior dev admits they've been stuck for three days but didn't want to look stupid. The political read on why the stakeholder keeps moving the goalposts.

These require trust. Context. Reading a room. Knowing when someone's "I'm fine" means "I'm drowning."

**The actual risk:**

Orgs will cut Scrum Masters, replace them with AI dashboards, and wonder why teams start underperforming six months later. By then, nobody will connect the dots.

## The Contrarian Take

If your value as a Scrum Master was "running meetings" and "updating Jira," you were already replaceable. By a spreadsheet.
