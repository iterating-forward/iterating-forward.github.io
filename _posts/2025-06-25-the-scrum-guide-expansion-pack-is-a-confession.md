**Summary**: Jeff Sutherland releases the "Scrum Guide Expansion Pack" - not a new version, but a supplement. It adds AI as an "actor" and introduces "Product Developers." What does this say about Scrum's direction?

**Tech Context (Jun 2025)**: June 11, 2025 - Scrum Guide Expansion Pack published. Introduces AI as actor, "Supporters" role, renames Developers to "Product Developers." Heavy focus on Cynefin and complexity.

---

When the creators of Scrum feel the need to "expand" their 13-page guide, it's worth asking: what problem are they solving?

## The Key Changes

1. **AI as an Actor**: Official recognition that AI tools are participants in development work
2. **Supporters**: People who help the team but aren't on the team
3. **Product Developers**: More than a name change - broader accountability for the whole product lifecycle

## What This Actually Means

The Scrum Guide was always a manifesto disguised as a manual. Thirteen pages to describe a complete development framework. Good luck.

Every CSM trainer I know spends half their course teaching things the Guide doesn't cover: how to actually run a retro, what a good Definition of Done looks like, how to handle dependencies between teams. The official answer was always "that's context-specific, figure it out."

Now Sutherland is filling in some blanks. Not by rewriting the Guide, but by adding an "expansion pack." The framing is clever. It's not an admission the Guide was incomplete. It's just... additional content. For the people who want it.

**The Supporters addition:**

This one matters. For years, the Guide pretended Scrum Teams could exist as self-contained units. In practice, every team relies on infrastructure engineers, UX researchers, legal reviewers, security consultants. People who help but don't sprint.

Calling them "Supporters" and giving them a name in the framework finally matches reality. It's not revolutionary. It's overdue.

**AI as an Actor:**

Less useful than it sounds. Yes, AI participates in development now. Copilot writes code. ChatGPT drafts documentation. But treating AI as an "actor" alongside humans creates weird accountability questions.

When the AI introduces a bug, who owns it? When the AI's suggestion violates compliance, who's responsible? The expansion pack doesn't answer these. It just says AI exists. Thanks.

**Product Developers:**

The renaming from "Developers" to "Product Developers" is meant to signal broader ownership. You're not just writing code. You're responsible for the product.

In practice, this changes nothing for teams that already worked this way, and won't change teams that didn't. A name in a document doesn't shift accountability. Culture does.

## The Contrarian Take

The most interesting addition isn't AI - it's "Supporters." Finally, official acknowledgment that teams need help from people who aren't on the team.
